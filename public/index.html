Books

Daddy's Disease

(Click cart to view on Amazon)

Designed to offer a young child a glimpse into the mind of an alcoholic, Daddy's Disease is a precious resource in helping children understand the difficulties of having an alcoholic parent. Follow Tommy on his journey through life, guided by his nurturing mother, as he learns how everyday situations relate to the difficulties his alcoholic father faces on a daily basis.

Insightful, poignant, and colorfully-illustrated, Daddy's Disease succeeds in honestly explaining a most serious subject manner through creative storytelling.

Mommy's Disease

(Click cart to view on Amazon)

Mommy's Disease, like Daddy's Disease, investigates substance abuse from the perspective of a child dealing with an alcoholic parent. Mila lives a normal life while attempting to cope with emotionally complex family matters. As Mila's mother struggles with addiction, Mila's father teaches the young girl to understand and accept, rather than be wounded by, her mother's failings.

A journey all it's own, Mommy's Disease offers pearls of wisdom and calming imagery to help a child understand the realities of addiction, simply and effectively.

About the Author

Carolyn Hannan Bell is a practicing licensed professional counselor in Cherry Hill, New Jersey.

She specializes in counseling those that suffer the adverse affects of substance abuse. Anxiety, depression, and codependency are all common reactions to dysfunctional relationships. In her practice, Carolyn uses cognitive behavioral techniques, years of experience in the field, and the occasional animal companion to help individuals overcome these and other crippling issues.

As an author, Carolyn's aim is to provide much-needed narratives for children and adults suffering in dysfunctional relationships. Each narrative is designed to give a more intuitive and simplistic understanding of some central issue. Carolyn hopes to empower children and families everywhere by shining a light into the darkness of emotional conflict.

Carolyn earned a Bachelors Degree in Psychology from Duquesne University and a Masters Degree in Counseling from Chestnut Hill College.
